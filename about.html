Why I started...

Powerlifting

As a young 6th grader, while surfing youtube, I came across a intriguing video: The one punch man challenge. The man in the video committed himself to running 10km, doing 100 pushups, 100 situps, and a 100 squats a day and in the process of doing so transformed his body. Deciding that I can do the same I decided to begin running 3 miles everyday, along with the same one punch man routine. Combined with Fraser WIlson’s 10 minute ab routine and a healthy diet, I began to see visible changes in my body. Albeit inefficient training, I found love for exercise and eventually set a personal best in the mile of 5 minutes and 30 seconds as a 6th grader.

Fast forward a couple months, while at a friend's house, I was exploring his home gym. I had seen videos of fitness influencers online, lifting weights, and I thought that I could do the same as well. While I was attempting to pick a weight off the ground any way I could, my friend’s brother walked in. He offered to help me learn how to lift weights correctly and my love for lifting weights began. I started lifting 3 times a week along with my running routine and gaining strength. Eventually, the workload proved to be too much, and I toned down running to focus on building muscle. I spent countless hours researching videos online to help with nutrition, and improve technique, and continued improving..

Covid hit and when the gyms were closed, I begged my dad to get home gym equipment. Though initially hesitant, his persistence resulted in us purchasing the essentials: a rack, a bar, and some bumper plates. While forced into quarantine, I continued to lift at home building up my lifts in a powerlifting style manner. Then, on a whim, I decided to sign up for a powerlifting meet, where the electric atmosphere captured me. I hope to keep hitting new personal records and challenging myself everyday.

Wrestling

My friend’s brother, who taught me how to lift, also took part in wrestling. Never have hearing about the sport before, I decided to give it a try. I googled “Dougherty Valley Wrestling” and searched for the head coach's email. Though slightly intimidated by the prospect, I sent the coach my interest in wrestling, as well as my current lifting stats. He emailed me back, inviting me to watch a practice. While visiting this practice, I saw a small room of about 10 wrestlers. Though it was a small room, all the wrestlers exuded passion and dedication, which I decided then that I wanted to be a part of. During the summer I joined their high-school summer program, where I began with the very basics. The first move I learned, a single leg, though seemingly simple, took me a long time to learn. Frustrated with my rate of progress, I bought a small 10 by 10 wrestling mat, which I used to practice what I learned at practice each day. Even though I tried my hardest, I ended the freshman season with a losing record, challenging my determination.

Through wrestling, the coaches taught me to never give up, and constantly seek to improve. This led me to join the wrestling program at the community youth center, where I further practiced their “three d’s”: discipline, dedication, and desire. Through my efforts in the offseason, I became the school’s first North Coast Section placer in over half-a-decade.

With the help of all my coaches, I continued to strive for improvement everyday. I hope introduce young individuals to the sport of wrestling as my friend’s brother did for me, as well as spread my knowledge that I have accumulated through the help of my coaches.
